<p><p>The inorganic microporous adsorbents market includes several key types: zeolites, cays, activated alumina, and silica gel. Zeolites are crystalline aluminosilicates known for their unique pore structure, making them effective for gas adsorption and separation. Cays, or clays, are natural minerals with microporous properties utilized in various applications. Activated alumina, with high surface area and porosity, is ideal for moisture removal and purification processes. Silica gel, a porous form of silica, is widely used for desiccation and as an adsorbent in diverse industries.</p></p>
