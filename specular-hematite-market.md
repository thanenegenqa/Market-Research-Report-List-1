<p><p>The specular hematite market is divided into two primary types: fines and pellets. Fines consist of small particles of hematite, typically used in the production of iron ore concentrates and in various industrial applications. Pellets, on the other hand, are spherical agglomerates made from fine hematite particles, designed for enhanced efficiency in steel manufacturing. Both types serve crucial roles in the steel industry, with fines primarily used in sintering processes and pellets favored for their improved handling and reduced emissions during smelting.</p></p>
